Research

Exploring Human-Machine Collaboration in Manufacturing

Current PhD Research

Recommender Systems for Human-Machine Collaboration in Manufacturing

Duration: November 2024 - October 2027
Supervisors: Prof. Markus Zanker and Prof. Angelika Peer
Industrial Partner: GKN Powder Metallurgy

My doctoral research focuses on developing intelligent recommender systems that enhance decision-making processes in manufacturing environments. The work explores how artificial intelligence can support human operators by providing personalized, context-aware recommendations that improve both efficiency and quality in industrial settings.

Research Questions

My research addresses several key questions in the intersection of artificial intelligence and manufacturing: How can Process-Aware Bayesian Networks effectively capture and represent operational knowledge from historical manufacturing decision logs? What are the optimal strategies for implementing Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) in dynamic manufacturing environments? How can recommender systems adapt to real-time process states while maintaining alignment with individual operator preferences? What factors influence the acceptance and effectiveness of AI-driven recommendations in human-machine collaborative scenarios?

Research Methodology

My research approach combines theoretical foundations with practical applications, employing a variety of methodologies to address complex problems in human-machine collaboration. I utilize data-driven analysis to identify patterns in large-scale manufacturing datasets, conduct controlled experiments to evaluate recommendation strategies, develop prototype systems to validate theoretical concepts, and perform user studies to understand human behavior and system acceptance in real-world industrial scenarios.

The interdisciplinary nature of my work requires careful integration of insights from computer science, industrial engineering, and human factors research. I emphasize reproducible research practices and open science principles, making my implementations and datasets available to the research community where possible.
