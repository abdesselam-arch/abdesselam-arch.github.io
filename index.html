Greetings! I am a PhD student specializing in Recommender Systems for Human-Machine Collaboration in Manufacturing at the Free University of Bozen-Bolzano, where I work in collaboration with GKN Powder Metallurgy. My research focuses on developing intelligent systems that enhance decision-making processes in industrial environments through personalized recommendations and adaptive learning algorithms.

My doctoral research explores the intersection of recommender systems, reinforcement learning, and human-machine interaction in manufacturing contexts. I am particularly interested in developing Process-Aware Bayesian Networks and implementing Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) to create adaptive recommendation systems that align with operator preferences and real-time process states.

Research

My research interests are in recommender systems, human-machine collaboration, and reinforcement learning. I work towards developing intelligent systems that enhance decision-making processes in manufacturing environments, with particular focus on creating adaptive, context-aware recommendation algorithms that align with human operator preferences while optimizing industrial processes.

Among all relevant topics, I am particularly interested in Process-Aware Bayesian Networks for knowledge representation, Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) for adaptive systems, explainable AI for building operator trust, and the broader challenges of human-machine collaboration in industrial settings. For more details, check out my publications and the research group's work.

Prospective collaborators: please feel free to reach out.
